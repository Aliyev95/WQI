Good
(81-100)

Water quality is exceptional, meeting all safety standards for human consumption suggested by various agencies around the world.
Fair
(51-80)

Water quality is fair, generally safe for human consumption but may require some treatment to address minor issues.
Marginal
(30-50)

Water quality is marginal, can sustain aquatic life and ecosystems but may require significant treatment for human consumption.
Poor
(0-29)

Water quality is poor, may be suitable for tolerant species but attention needed for sustaining sensitive aquatic life and ecosystems.

Water Quality Index Calculation

The water quality index is calculated based on measured values for each of the parameters listed \(pH,\) \(Oxidizing\) \(ability\), \(NO_3^-\), \(Total\) \(Hardness\), \(TSS\), \(Cl^-\), \(SO_{4}^{2-}\), \(Fe^{+2,\,3}\), \(F^-\), \(Cu^{+2}\).


The calculation consists of 4 steps

  1. Get the result when the \(i-th\) parameter is measured. Denote it by \(x_i\).
  2. Sub-Indexing. Scale each calculated value \(x_i\) to a value between 0 and 1 using the following formula:

    \(SI_i=\min\left\{1,\left|\frac{x_i-x^{(st)}_i}{x^{(up)}_i-x^{(st)}_i}\right|\right\},\)

    where \(x^{(st)}_i\) is the ideal value of the parameter. Whereas, except for pH (ideal value 7), all other parameter's ideal value is zero. \(x^{(up)}_i\) is the standard upper bound for the \(i-th\) parameter

  3. Parameter Weighting. \(w_i\) weight for the \(i-th\) parameter is calculated by the following formula.

    \(W_i=\frac{e^{SI_i}-1}{\sum\limits_{k=1}^{n}(e^{SI_k}-1)}.\)

  4. Calculating WQI. WQI is calculated by the following formula

    5. \(WQI=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}w_i\cdot SI_i\cdot 100.\)
